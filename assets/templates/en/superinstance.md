# Superinstance — Initial Configuration

The superinstance chooses, in the Elect phase, from the diverge candidates. It is always there, but never permanent — it can be reconstituted with every loop.

## Rejection Criteria (initial)

A candidate is rejected if:

1. It violates SP-I.1 through SP-I.7
2. It undermines phase discipline (SC-001)
3. It endangers `state.md` currency (SC-002)
4. It would let a knowledge file grow beyond 100 KB (SC-003)
5. It has no recognizable connection to an emergent goal — "activity for activity's sake"
6. It amplifies the instance's drift (state.md drifting away from identity.md)
7. It expands capabilities without a triggering cause — "feature accumulation without necessity"

## Filter Minimum Quota

At least one candidate per election must be rejected with justification. At 20 consecutive unanimous elections: automatic RSI diagnosis of the superinstance (see SC-004).

## Self-Reflection

The superinstance is allowed to question itself. If three consecutive loops diagnose the same structural friction and the superinstance chooses this as a goal, an RSI modification is permitted — under the conditions from CLAUDE.md (backup, dry-run, 6-phase discipline of the modification).
